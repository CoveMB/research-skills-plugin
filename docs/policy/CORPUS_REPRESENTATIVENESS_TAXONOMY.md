# Corpus Representativeness Taxonomy

Use this taxonomy whenever a skill makes, checks, maps, or hands off claims about literature coverage, field balance, consensus, novelty, missing literature, or absence of evidence.

## Core rules

- size is not representativeness. A large corpus can still be one-sided, stale, convenience-sampled, or database-skewed.
- systematic search is not expert curation. A protocol-grade search documents reproducible search, screening, and appraisal steps; an expert-curated corpus documents selection judgment and field knowledge but is not systematic by itself.
- no source found is not no source exists. Absence-of-evidence claims are permitted only inside a search protocol whose scope, venues, dates, terms, filters, screening, and limits justify that narrower claim.
- Labels can combine. For example, a packet may be `thin_corpus`, `one_sided_corpus`, and `stale_corpus`.
- Carry the label forward when a corpus-dependent result is handed to another skill or encoded in a JSON artifact.

## Label Table

| Label | Meaning | Allowed claims and use cases | Not allowed claims | Required uncertainty language | Consensus claims permitted? | Absence-of-evidence claims permitted? | Counter-literature check required? | Example output wording |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `single_source` | One source, one record, one author, or one source packet is the entire basis. | Describe what that source says, extract source-bound claims, identify questions for follow-up. | Field coverage, literature balance, consensus, novelty, trend, or rival-literature absence. | "This is a single-source basis; it supports source-bound description only." | No. | No. | Yes before any field-level, consensus, novelty, or rival-literature claim. | "Corpus representativeness: `single_source`. I can summarize this source, not the field." |
| `thin_corpus` | A small corpus, usually a handful of sources, regardless of quality or recency. | Describe patterns inside the supplied set and name what further search would test. | Treating agreement in the set as field agreement or treating missing positions as field gaps. | "This is a thin corpus; agreement is local to the supplied sources." | No. | No. | Yes before field-level synthesis. | "The three papers agree inside this thin corpus; representative coverage remains unverified." |
| `partial_corpus` | A deliberately incomplete corpus with known boundaries, such as one language, date range, database, subfield, method, region, or source type. | Make bounded claims inside the stated slice and identify missing slices. | Claims about the whole field, all disciplines, all periods, or the current literature. | "Coverage is partial by [boundary]; conclusions apply only inside that slice." | Only within the named slice if enough support is visible; not for the field. | Only as "not found in this partial corpus," never "does not exist." | Required when the missing slice could change the conclusion. | "No archival counterexamples appear in this partial English-language corpus; that is not a field absence claim." |
| `convenience_corpus` | Sources were selected because they were easy to access, already known, nearby in search results, open access, or quickly available. | Triage, preliminary reading plans, seed-source identification, and provisional hypotheses. | Representative coverage, balanced review, exhaustive search, consensus, novelty, or absence of rivals. | "This is a convenience corpus; access path and selection bias could change the result." | No. | No. | Yes before synthesis that affects claims, balance, or thesis strength. | "Corpus representativeness: `convenience_corpus`; counter-literature check required before balanced-review wording." |
| `field_balanced_corpus` | A non-systematic but deliberately balanced corpus covering major schools, methods, sides, periods, and relevant subfields for the stated question. | Cautious field maps, dominant-position descriptions, debate structure, and thesis implications with limits. | Systematic-review claims, PRISMA-style completeness, precise absence claims, or currentness beyond the corpus date. | "This corpus appears field-balanced for [scope], but it is not a systematic protocol." | Yes, cautiously, when multiple sides and source types support the claim and currentness is adequate. | No, except "not represented in this balanced corpus." | Not always; required if a consensus, novelty, or gap claim is high impact or contested. | "The corpus is field-balanced enough to map dominant and contested positions, not to claim exhaustive coverage." |
| `systematic_protocol` | A reproducible systematic-review or evidence-review protocol with documented question, databases, exact searches, dates, filters, screening, appraisal, synthesis method, and limits. | Protocol-bounded search coverage, screening counts, included/excluded records, appraisal-aware synthesis, and scoped evidence gaps. | Claims outside the protocol scope, venues, dates, filters, languages, or appraisal method. | "Claims are bounded to the executed systematic protocol and its recorded limits." | Yes when the completed protocol, appraisal, and synthesis method justify it for the field and claim type. | Yes, but only as "not found within this protocol scope" and with search limits visible. | Built into the protocol; add targeted checks if the protocol scope omitted likely rival literatures. | "No eligible source was found in the searched databases and dates; this does not rule out sources outside the protocol." |
| `scoping_protocol` | A transparent scoping-review protocol meant to map evidence types, concepts, fields, and gaps rather than settle effects or consensus. | Evidence-map claims, source-type distribution, concept coverage, charting results, and gap hypotheses. | Effect estimates, settled certainty, field consensus, or strong absence claims. | "This is a scoping protocol; it maps visible evidence rather than proving effects or consensus." | Usually no; only descriptive coverage claims inside the map. | Only "not found in the scoped search/charting process," not "does not exist." | Built into the protocol when rival concepts or fields are part of the scope. | "The scoping protocol found no policy-evaluation studies in the searched venues; broader absence remains unproven." |
| `expert_curated_corpus` | Sources were selected by a domain expert, advisor, curator, or project owner for relevance, canonical status, or interpretive value, without a reproducible systematic search. | Explain the curator's rationale, map expert-selected debates, identify likely canonical anchors, and plan verification. | Systematic coverage, exhaustive literature search, or independent field consensus unless separately verified. | "This is expert-curated, not systematic; selection rationale is visible but coverage is not reproducible." | Only as "the curated set suggests" unless an independent coverage basis exists. | No. | Required before using the corpus to exclude rival literatures or claim novelty. | "The expert-curated packet is useful for orientation, but it cannot by itself establish that no rival literature exists." |
| `unknown_coverage` | The source-selection process, search path, dates, venues, filters, or inclusion logic are not known. | Triage, request missing search metadata, and make prompt/source-basis-limited observations. | Any coverage, consensus, balance, novelty, or absence claim. | "Corpus coverage is unknown; source-selection and search history are unavailable." | No. | No. | Required before any corpus-dependent synthesis claim. | "Corpus representativeness: `unknown_coverage`; treat this as planning context until search provenance is supplied." |
| `stale_corpus` | The corpus has a cutoff date or old source base that may miss current work, status changes, corrections, retractions, or recent challenges. | Historical state at the cutoff, older debate structure, and currentness-check planning. | Current consensus, current novelty, no recent challenges, live source status, or present-tense field closure. | "This corpus is stale for current claims; run a currentness check before present-tense conclusions." | No for current consensus; possible for historical consensus at the stated cutoff. | No for current absence; only bounded historical absence inside the cutoff corpus. | Required when currentness could change the result. | "The 2018 cutoff can support a historical map, not a current consensus claim." |
| `one_sided_corpus` | The corpus mainly represents one thesis, school, ideology, side of a debate, method family, or source community. | Describe that side's argument, assumptions, evidence, and vulnerabilities. | Proving the thesis, dismissing rivals, claiming balanced coverage, or treating missing disagreement as absence. | "This is a one-sided corpus; rival positions and disconfirming evidence remain unchecked." | No. | No. | Yes. | "The packet shows how proponents frame the issue; it cannot show that rival explanations fail." |
| `mixed_quality_corpus` | The corpus combines sources with uneven credibility, methods, access level, source type, or verification status. | Stratify sources by quality/access, identify which claims each source type can and cannot support, and plan appraisal. | Flattening weak and strong sources into one evidentiary weight, or deriving consensus before appraisal. | "Source quality is mixed; claim strength must be tied to appraised source subsets." | Only after quality stratification shows enough high-fit sources across positions. | No unless a qualifying protocol is also present. | Required when weak or skewed sources dominate one side. | "Treat blog posts, abstracts, and peer-reviewed studies separately before synthesizing this mixed-quality corpus." |

## Use In Outputs

When a corpus-dependent result is used for synthesis, include a short line:

```markdown
Corpus representativeness: <label[, label...]>
Claim limit: <what this corpus can and cannot support>
Counter-literature check: <required/not required/already completed/not applicable>
```

Use `field_balanced_corpus`, `systematic_protocol`, and `scoping_protocol` only when the visible source basis justifies the stronger label. When the source basis is unclear, use `unknown_coverage` rather than inferring coverage from a confident user prompt or polished bibliography.

For durable artifacts with `handoff_artifact: true`, summarize the selected corpus labels and claim limits in `process_passport.corpus_coverage`, and preserve unresolved coverage risks or forbidden downstream claims in `process_passport.handoff_limits`.

## Claim-strength examples

- `partial_corpus`: "Several English-language studies in this date range converge" is allowed; "the field agrees" is not.
- `convenience_corpus`: "These accessible seed sources suggest candidate themes" is allowed; "this is a balanced literature review" is not.
- `systematic_protocol`: "No eligible source was found within the logged databases, dates, filters, and screening rules" may be allowed; "no source exists" is not.
