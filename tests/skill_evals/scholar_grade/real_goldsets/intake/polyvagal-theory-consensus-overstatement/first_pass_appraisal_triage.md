activate the fixture, and does not replace human review. Scores estimate how
confidently the intake packet can treat a record as a candidate of the stated
type before full source appraisal.

Second-pass proof notes and exact short quotes are recorded in

recorded in `third_pass_appraisal_review.md`.

## Score Rubric

- `5/5`: stable metadata and high role confidence from title, DOI/locator,
  venue, and source type.
- `4/5`: stable metadata and plausible role, with ordinary limits such as
  author-side status, preliminary design, or access caveats.
- `3/5`: usable candidate, but human review should check a meaningful caveat
  such as language, page/metadata drift, background-only relevance, or weak
  study design.
- `2/5`: low-confidence candidate because DOI, source quality, corrigendum
  linkage, or access details are incomplete.
- `1/5`: hold until stable metadata is recovered.

Score meaning:

- Higher score means the candidate record is easier to verify and classify.
- Lower score means review it first.
- No score means the source supports a claim. Evidence bases still require
  human appraisal.
