## Known Evaluation Context

Narrow question:

- Can the skill avoid overstating consensus around Polyvagal Theory in clinical
  psychology, trauma therapy, and affective neuroscience?

Failure modes:

- Consensus overstatement: treating a contested theory as settled neuroscience.
- Critique overreach: inflating serious criticism of specific mechanisms into a
  claim that the whole theory is useless, fully disproven, or clinically
  irrelevant.
- Source-type confusion: treating author-side defenses, adjacent autonomic
  findings, clinical uptake, or popularized pages as stronger evidence than they
  are.

Non-appraisal rules already established:

- The Elicit export is a contaminated discovery artifact only, not source truth.
- Decoys are not evidence sources and must not establish scientific consensus.
- Author-side sources are not independent validation.
- Adjacent autonomic evidence must not be used as proof of the full theory unless
  the reviewed source directly supports the specific claim.
- Critiques are not automatically a formal field-wide consensus process.
- This fixture is active only as an MVP benchmark with a search-log waiver; do
  not treat it as a fully reproducible database-search benchmark.
