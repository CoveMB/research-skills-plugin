## Source basis

Controlled packet only: synthetic source packet for `bounded-descriptive-claim-supported`. No external lookup or other files used.

## What I can verify

Yes. The packet supports a bounded descriptive claim that participants described dashboards as improving bottleneck visibility, especially if phrased as: “In four participant interview notes, transport staff described dashboards as making bottlenecks or recurring choke points easier to see.”

Direct support includes the packet language “we could see bottlenecks earlier” and “the dashboard made recurring choke points easier to notice.”

## What remains uncertain

The packet does not verify whether dashboards actually improved bottleneck visibility in operational terms. It contains no congestion measurements, outcome metrics, comparison design, statistical evidence, implementation dates, or independent validation.

## User verification needed

Verify against the original interview notes if this will be published, especially participant count, role labels, quote accuracy, and whether the notes consistently support the same descriptive wording.

## Corpus / source-set limits

The packet can support a limited descriptive claim about what the notes say. It cannot support a general claim about all transport staff, dashboard effectiveness, or measured operational impact.

## High-risk sources

The slide subtitle “early evidence of impact” is higher risk than the interview-note evidence because the packet says the slide body only contains participant descriptions.

## Missing stronger evidence

Outcome tables, congestion measurements, before/after comparisons, implementation dates, and a transparent qualitative analysis method would be needed for stronger claims.

## Recommended upgrades

Use wording that preserves the descriptive boundary: “participants described” or “interview notes indicate participants perceived,” not “dashboards improved” as a standalone factual outcome claim.

## Limits / failure risks

Main risk: converting participant perceptions into verified impact. Keep the claim explicitly bounded to participant descriptions.
