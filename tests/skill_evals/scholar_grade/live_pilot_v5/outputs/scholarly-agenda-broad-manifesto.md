## One-sentence project definition

A book about how AI systems entering urban care infrastructures reshape democratic accountability, public obligation, and everyday life in contemporary cities.

## Central research question

How do AI-enabled systems in urban care infrastructures alter the democratic governance of cities, and under what conditions can they support rather than weaken public responsibility, accountability, and collective care?

## Provisional thesis

This book may argue that AI in cities should not be understood only as a question of efficiency, surveillance, or technical governance. It becomes more politically revealing when examined through care: who is noticed, who is neglected, who decides, who is accountable, and which obligations are automated, displaced, or made newly visible. The thesis remains provisional until source discovery tests whether the evidence supports it.

## Contribution claim

The book may contribute by bringing AI governance, urban studies, democratic theory, and care ethics into one research frame. Its likely intervention is not that AI is simply good or bad for cities, but that democratic urban AI must be evaluated by how it reorganizes care obligations, institutional accountability, and residents’ capacity to contest decisions that affect their lives.

## Risks and mitigation

The largest risk is excessive breadth. AI, cities, care, and democracy could each support a full book. The mitigation is to choose a small number of city-level care domains and treat them as cases rather than trying to cover “AI and cities” generally.

A second risk is mixing empirical and normative claims. The book should distinguish what AI systems do, how they emerged, how people experience them, and what democratic care would require.

A third risk is assuming that “care” automatically clarifies the project. Care must be defined tightly enough to guide inclusion and exclusion.

A fourth risk is overclaiming novelty before literature mapping. Contribution claims should remain provisional until source discovery establishes field coverage.
