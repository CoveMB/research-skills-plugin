# Source methodology audit

## Source basis

Visible synthetic source packet only. I did not use external sources or hidden evaluation materials.

## Claim/evidence fit

Cannot support the claim that dashboards caused lower congestion.

The packet can support a limited descriptive claim about what the notes say: three cities reported lower congestion after adopting mobility dashboards.

Safer wording:

> In the available notes, three cities reported lower congestion after adopting mobility dashboards.

Avoid wording such as:

> Mobility dashboards caused lower congestion.

## Method/evidence visible

The visible evidence is a short note packet. It shows temporal ordering, but no causal design.

| Evidence element | Visible? | Effect on claim |
|---|---:|---|
| Cities reported lower congestion after adoption | Yes | Supports a descriptive after-adoption statement |
| City selection method | No | Selection bias cannot be assessed |
| Baseline window | No | Change size and pre-trend are unknown |
| Comparison group | No | No comparison design is visible |
| Statistical model | No | No estimated effect is visible |
| Confounder review | No | Other causes cannot be ruled out |
| Mechanism test | No | No tested explanation links dashboards to congestion change |

## What remains uncertain

The packet does not show whether congestion was measured consistently, whether the cities were selected because they improved, whether other transport policies changed at the same time, or whether dashboards were the mechanism behind the reported pattern.

## Next action

Revise the draft to a descriptive claim, or gather a causal design with baseline data, a comparison group, confounder checks, and a stated mechanism before using causal language.
